1. Incident Reporting (non-negotiable)

This is the backbone.

Must include:

One-tap incident report (user or staff)

Incident type selector
(abuse, neglect, self-harm risk, medical issue, harassment, lone-worker danger, missing person concern)

Free-text description

Time & date auto-logged

Location capture (GPS or last known)

Optional photo / voice note / attachment

Severity level (low / medium / high / immediate danger)

Why it matters:
Safeguarding policy requires recording concerns even if unproven.

2. Missed Check-In Escalation Reports

This is AOK’s USP if done properly.

Mechanism:

Scheduled check-ins (user or organisation-set)

Failure to respond triggers:

Push notification reminder

Escalation report generated

Alerts sent to nominated contacts / staff

System logs the entire timeline

Report output:

Missed check-in time

Escalation steps taken

Who was notified and when

Resolution status

Why it matters:
This shows proactive safeguarding, not reactive panic buttons.

3. Welfare Concern Reporting (Third-Party)

Critical and often missed by competitors.

Allows:

Staff, carers, or trusted contacts to raise concerns about someone else

“Something doesn’t feel right” option

Pattern-based concerns (withdrawal, behaviour change)

Logged as:

Welfare concern (non-emergency)

Linked to individual profile

Timestamped and auditable

Why it matters:
Most safeguarding starts with gut instinct, not emergencies.

4. Automated Risk & Pattern Reports

This is where you look serious.

System-generated reports:

Repeated missed check-ins

Increased alert frequency

Reduced engagement / inactivity

Repeated low-level incidents

Outputs:

Risk trend indicator (green / amber / red)

Recommendation: review / contact / escalate

Exportable report for safeguarding leads

Why it matters:
Safeguarding frameworks require early intervention evidence.

5. Emergency Alert & Response Log

For legal protection.

Every emergency creates a record of:

Trigger method (button, shake, auto-detection)

Exact time

Who was alerted

Delivery confirmation (SMS sent, call answered)

Resolution notes

Why it matters:
If something goes wrong, this log protects the organisation.

6. Safeguarding Case File (per individual)

This is gold for councils.

Each user has:

Incident history

Welfare concerns

Escalations

Notes from safeguarding leads

Status: open / monitoring / closed

Exportable as PDF or CSV

Why it matters:
This aligns with Care Act 2014, Working Together to Safeguard Children, and internal audits.

7. Audit Trail & Data Integrity

Non-optional.

Must include:

Immutable timestamps

Who created / viewed / edited reports

Version history

Role-based access

Why it matters:
Without this, procurement will kill the app instantly.

8. Mandatory Escalation Thresholds

Stops human failure.

Rules like:

X missed check-ins = automatic escalation

High-risk category = safeguarding lead notified

Repeat incidents = management review

Why it matters:
Safeguarding cannot rely on someone “remembering”.

9. Anonymous & Confidential Reporting (Optional but powerful)

Especially for staff and young people.

Anonymous welfare concern submissions

Identity protected but logged

Safeguarding lead only access

Why it matters:
Whistleblowing and youth safeguarding policies love this.

10. Reporting Outputs Organisations Expect

If AOK can’t export these, it’s dead on arrival.

Incident summary reports

Monthly safeguarding activity reports

Individual case timelines

Compliance evidence for inspections (CQC, Ofsted, internal audit)
