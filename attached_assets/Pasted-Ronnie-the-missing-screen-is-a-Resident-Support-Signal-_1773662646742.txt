Good question. If someone presses “Need Support” or “Urgent Help”, relying on someone watching a dashboard is not acceptable for safeguarding. The system must actively push alerts.

You need multi-channel escalation, not passive monitoring.

1. Immediate Staff Notification (Primary Layer)

When a resident sends a signal, the system instantly pushes alerts to the assigned staff.

Channels:

Push notification (staff mobile app)

SMS

Email

Example alert staff receive:

A-OK Support Signal

Resident: Sarah K
Location: Unit 12
Status: Needs Support
Time: 14:22

Tap to respond

Push notifications should be the main mechanism.

2. Escalation Timer (If No One Responds)

You must build response timers.

Example flow:

Resident presses "Need Support"
↓
Alert sent to assigned support worker
↓
If no response in 5 minutes
↓
Alert escalates to service manager
↓
If still no response in 10 minutes
↓
Alert escalates to on-call staff

For Urgent Help, escalation should be faster.

Example:

Urgent Help pressed
↓
Instant alert to all on-site staff
↓
Escalation to manager within 2 minutes
3. On-Site Visual Alert (Optional but Powerful)

If this is a hostel or supported housing building, you can show a live alert panel.

Example screen at reception:

Active Alerts

Sarah K – Needs Support – 14:22

That ensures staff inside the building see it.

4. Automatic Timeline Logging

Every step must be recorded automatically.

Example timeline:

14:22 – Support signal triggered
14:24 – Alert sent to staff
14:26 – Staff responded
14:28 – Support conversation logged

This becomes safeguarding evidence.

5. Staff Acknowledge the Alert

Staff should tap “Responding”.

This stops escalation.

Example flow:

Alert received
↓
Staff taps "Responding"
↓
Escalation stops
↓
Interaction logged after visit
6. Offline or System Failure Backup

You also need a fallback rule for safeguarding.

Example protocol:

If system unavailable
↓
Residents use staff desk / emergency number
↓
Staff log interaction later

Services will ask this question during pilots.

7. Why This Matters for Your Demo

When you show the system, you must demonstrate this flow clearly:

Resident presses "Need Support"
↓
Staff phone alert
↓
Staff responds
↓
Interaction logged
↓

That shows the complete safeguarding loop.

One Important Safeguarding Rule

The system must never create false expectations.

Meaning the resident interface should clearly say something like:

“This is not an emergency service. If you are in immediate danger contact emergency services.”

Most safeguarding systems include this.
