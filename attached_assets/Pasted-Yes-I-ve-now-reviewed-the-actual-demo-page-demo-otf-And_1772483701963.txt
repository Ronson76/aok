✅ Short Answer (Straight off the fence)

It’s not “bad” — but it does overcomplicate things if the intention is to repeat this for every organisation.

Right now it looks like a bespoke pilot showcase, not a scalable organisational product.

And that distinction matters a lot.

⸻

🧠 What I Think Your Dad’s Intention Likely Is

Reading the structure and tone, this page feels like it’s trying to do one of these:

Likely intention:

👉 Show Off The Fence a tangible vision of how AOK integrates with an organisation’s ecosystem.

In other words:
	•	“Here’s what partnership could look like”
	•	“Here’s your people inside AOK”
	•	“Here’s your branded experience”

This is actually very common in early pilots.

It helps the first partner emotionally buy in.

So strategically, for a pilot, this makes sense.

⸻

🚨 The Problem (Your Concern Is Valid)

If this becomes the model going forward, you create a massive operational problem:

You’d be building:

AOK x Organisation A page
AOK x Organisation B page
AOK x Organisation C page
AOK x Organisation D page
...

That quickly becomes:

❌ unmaintainable
❌ inconsistent safeguarding messaging
❌ expensive to update
❌ confusing for commissioners

And most importantly:

👉 It shifts AOK from platform/provider to custom web agency.

⸻

🔎 What the Page Currently Feels Like to an Organisation

From a professional safeguarding/commissioning lens, the page reads as:

“A customised experience designed specifically for Off The Fence.”

That creates two unintended signals:

1️⃣ Other organisations may assume:

“We need our own version built.”

Which raises friction immediately.

⸻

2️⃣ Commissioners may wonder:

“Is this a structured model or just marketing?”

Organisations trust repeatable systems, not bespoke presentations.

⸻

⭐ The Core Strategic Question

You need to decide whether AOK is:

OPTION A — Partnership Showcase Model

Each organisation gets a tailored microsite.

Pros
	•	Feels personal
	•	Strong pilot engagement

Cons
	•	Not scalable
	•	Hard governance control
	•	Messaging drift
	•	Maintenance nightmare

⸻

OPTION B — Platform Model (What scales)

Instead of:

“Off The Fence version of AOK”

You present:

“AOK safeguarding & engagement platform used by Off The Fence.”

This is a huge psychological difference.

⸻

✅ The Better Approach (What Mature Organisations Expect)

Keep ONE core structure.

Then lightly personalise.

⸻

Instead of custom pages, do this:

Core page (same for everyone):

How Organisations Work With AOK

Then include:
	•	Case study: Off The Fence (pilot partner)
	•	Example outcomes
	•	Example workflow

Off The Fence becomes proof, not a template.

⸻

Personalisation should be SMALL, not structural:

Things that can vary safely:
	•	Logo badge (“Working with Off The Fence”)
	•	Named contacts
	•	Programme name
	•	Referral link

Things that should NEVER change:
	•	safeguarding model
	•	governance explanation
	•	process flow
	•	risk framework
	•	referral logic

⸻

🧩 Why This Matters (Big Insight)

Organisations are not buying a website experience.

They are buying:

✅ predictable safeguarding
✅ repeatable process
✅ accountable structure

If every organisation looks different, it subconsciously signals:

“The system itself may change.”

That creates risk anxiety.

⸻

🚨 The Hidden Risk You Might Not See Yet

If pilots succeed and you onboard 5–10 partners:

You’ll suddenly need to update:
	•	safeguarding wording
	•	policy changes
	•	referral flows
	•	contact routes

…across multiple custom pages.

That becomes governance risk — not just workload.

⸻

💡 What I’d Recommend (Best of Both Worlds)

Keep the OTF page — but reposition its purpose.

Rename internally as:

👉 Pilot Demonstration Environment

Not a production model.

Then convert it into:

Case Study: Off The Fence Pilot

Show:
	•	challenge
	•	solution
	•	how AOK integrated
	•	early outcomes

Now it strengthens credibility instead of creating expectation.
